Overview of Wildlife in India

India is home to an incredibly diverse array of wildlife, owing to its varied climates and landscapes. From the lush rainforests of the Western Ghats and the northeastern states to the arid deserts of Rajasthan and the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, each habitat supports unique ecosystems.

The country boasts a rich array of flora and fauna, with over 90,000 species of animals and around 45,000 species of plants. India is particularly renowned for its large mammal species, including the Bengal tiger, Indian elephant, Indian rhinoceros, and Asiatic lion. Additionally, the nation hosts numerous bird species, reptiles, amphibians, and a vibrant array of marine life.

India's wildlife is protected in over 500 wildlife sanctuaries and 100 national parks, ensuring that future generations can continue to enjoy and benefit from this natural heritage. Iconic protected areas include Jim Corbett National Park, Ranthambore National Park, and Kaziranga National Park, each offering unique opportunities to experience wildlife up close.

Conservation efforts are ongoing, focusing on habitat preservation, anti-poaching initiatives, and community involvement in wildlife protection. These efforts are crucial in maintaining the delicate balance of India's ecosystems and safeguarding its diverse wildlife for the future.
